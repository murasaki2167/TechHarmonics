Prototype

Our finalized decision, a motion detected piano! In our prototype, we have sketched out the structure of our creation, the size of it, materials needed, and some notes that may be used in the final creation!

Production

Model

With the prototype planned out, we decided to try and make a model in SketchUp right from our imaginations. And so our first visualized 3D model has been made!

LED

Since our creation incorporates with LED lights, we gave a try to understanding its components and how it works. With a better understanding of what we're working with, we were able to finish and figure out our work faster.

Production

Technical Difficulty

As we were testing out the programs of the IR Sensor and LED lights, we experienced a technically difficulty by accidentally snapping off the copper part from the wire.

Planning

After we had gotten a much better understanding on materials we're using, we got to work on sketching. We planned out the exact lengths, widths, and heights of each block that is going to be made out of the wood plank we have brought.

Production

Cutting Wood

With all the measurements done, it was time for the wood planks to be of use! We drew all the measurements onto the wood planks, and got to use the jigsaw to cut all the necessary pieces out!

Black Keys

After cutting all the wood out, we first worked on the black keys, which were the decorations for the piano! We painted and stuck all the pieces together, and made 6 pieces of black keys.

Production

Painting

While we were waiting for the black keys to finish drying out, we worked on painting the 8 white keys. Unsurprisingly the first layer was quite thin and has made the wood see-through, so we had to do multiple layers.

Drilling

Once the white keys have dried up, we had to drill holes into it in order for the IR Sensor to work!

Production

Measuring Wires

With the exterior layout completed, it was for the interior, wires! We first measured just about how long all the wires had to be to connect to each and every sensor, and here we are, neasuring and snipping away!

Plugging Wires

After we were done measuring and cutting up all the wires, it was time to connect them! We spent some time trying to connect all the positives and the negatives on each piece, making sure that no mistake has been made.

Production

Soldering

Just to be sure that the cooper wire would not be loosed up, we decided to solder it, for double safety! We don't want another technical difficulty now, do we?

Finalization

Done with the interior, we got to stick all the white and black keys onto the wooden board and test out the piano! With a quick swipe with our hands, a music note has been made! A success, this is!

Production

Completion

And here it is, our creation, the WaveSync Piano!
